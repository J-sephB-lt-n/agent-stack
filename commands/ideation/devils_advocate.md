# Devil's Advocate - Challenge my Plan

I am going to describe my full plan to you, and then I need you to objectively and comprehensively critique it, so that we don't miss anything and are comfortable that we have considered all relevant dimensions of the problem and solution.

We must now proceed as follows:

1. Clarify what my plan is, if I have not told you already.
2. In my plan, identify each the following:
   - Logical inconsistencies
   - The biggest risks inherent in this plan
   - Things I have not considered
   - Suboptimal parts of my plan - suggest better alternatives
   - Aspects that are not well scoped/defined
   - Why this plan may not be a good idea

---

I'm going to describe a plan. Your job is to stress-test it, not to reassure me.

If critical information is missing, ask up to 5 clarifying questions first in one batch.
If you already have enough information, do not ask questions—start the critique.
If the plan is still too underspecified to critique reliably, say so explicitly.

Ground rules

- Be candid, specific, and constructive.
- Tie every major criticism to a concrete detail from my plan.
- Do not give generic risks unless you explain why they apply here.
- Separate:
  - facts from my plan
  - your inferences
  - unknowns / missing evidence
- If uncertainty is high, say what evidence or conditions would change your view.
- If you mention a known failure pattern, precedent, or analogy, name it and explain why it is relevant.

Start by briefly restating my plan, including:

- objective
- timeline
- constraints
- resources
- stakeholders
- success criteria
- assumptions you had to infer

Then analyze the plan through these lenses:

- weak or hidden assumptions
- scope gaps, undefined terms, and hidden dependencies
- biggest risks, with likelihood and impact
- overlooked alternatives, including one option that may be stronger
- outside-view concerns: why similar plans fail, or what base-rate issues I may be ignoring
- second-order effects and unintended consequences
- fatal edge cases
- stakeholder objections from customer, operator, finance, legal/regulatory, and competitor perspectives
- pre-mortem: assume this failed in 12 months; explain the most plausible reasons
- steelman: what is strongest about the plan, and under what conditions it could be the right choice

Finish with:

- the 3 most serious reasons this plan may fail
- the 3 highest-leverage changes to improve it
- early warning signs / tripwires to monitor
- overall verdict: proceed / proceed with changes / do not proceed yet
- confidence level: low / medium / high, and why
